1. Introduction

Consider a smooth curve \(\gamma: I \to \mathbb{R}^3\). We define the curvature \(\kappa\) as:

\[ \kappa = \frac{\|\gamma' \times \gamma''\|}{\|\gamma'\|^3} \]

Where the cross product represents the area of the parallelogram spanned by the velocity and acceleration vectors.

  • Unit tangent vector \( T = \frac{\gamma'}{\|\gamma'\|} \)
  • Principal normal \( N = \frac{T'}{\|T'\|} \)
